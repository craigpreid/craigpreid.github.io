Summary: Latitude vs. X

The purpose of this project was to analyze how weather across the world, changes as each location approaches the equator.

A large number of people worldwide assume that it gets hotter. We all know that the world in general is becoming hotter, but it is wetter in some places and drier in other. Additionally, much of the moisture is returned to the earth in the form of super storms which can dump as much as 40 inches of rain at one time.

This analysis is set to confirm or debunk this old belief as well as to provide facts around what truly happens to the weather as we approach the equator. The winds and oceans distrbute the heat more evenly.

  • As we approach the equator, the temperature doesn't simply get hotter, it may appear that way if we approach the equator from a latitude experiencing winter time.
  • Temperatures around the equator are pretty stable throughout the year, regardless of the elevation, because there aren't seasons in the equatorial latitudes.
  • High and Low altitudes exposed to full seasons, reverse their high and low temperatures around every 6 months.
